Lois Ogilvie Blanchette – a musician, composer,
arranger and conductress.
Currently 95 and residing in Sherbrooke, Quebec, Mrs. Blanchette’s musical career spanned nine decades. After completing a Modern languages degree at the University of Ottawa and a music degree at the Toronto Conservatory, Mrs. Blanchette became the soloist colouratura soprano in the Leslie Bell Singers. The choir, directed by Dr. Leslie Bell, was the most prominent female a cappella group from the 1930s to the 1950s. Mrs. Blanchette married in 1950 and moved to Sherbrooke with her husband and judge, Guy Blanchette. During the next six decades Mrs. Blanchette conducted eight different choirs. Her most famous choir was La Chorale de l’Amitié or The Singing Girls . La Chorale de l’Amitié, which was also a ladies’ a cappella choir, travelled around Ontario and Quebec to perform concerts. In 1967, they sang at Expo 67 where they won first prize. Over her conducting career, Mrs. Blanchette arranged over 150 pieces and composed 70. She is accomplished in the violin, piano, harp and voice, and has learned seven languages.
